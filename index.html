    It is my pleasure to express my gratitude to the instructor for teaching project management skills to me as a third-year student at Astana Information Technology University. I can vouch for the fact that I have gained all of the necessary talents.
    When it comes to project management, it is critical to make use of the process's experience and capabilities in order to ensure that the team completes the project on time, within budget, and according to schedule. One of the key responsibilities of project management is to ensure that all of the project's objectives are met within the timeframe specified. As a result, the project manager must anticipate as many challenges and barriers as possible in order to avoid delays. He is ultimately responsible for the project's successful initiation, planning, design, execution, monitoring, control, and completion. He is also responsible for the project's successful conclusion.
    When it comes to project management, it differs depending on whether you are a manager in a large corporation, a professional in this field, or a student eager to enter the field of project management. However, there are three fundamental axioms that should be followed by any project manager, regardless of their position:

  • Create project plans for your business.
  • Organizing and characterizing a situation
  • Creating and maintaining realistic expectations
  • Tasks should be organized.
  • Resource allocation is being planned.
  • Estimation of the Time and Money Required
  • Analysis and management of risks and problems
  • Monitoring and reporting on the progress of the project are essential.

    • During the project management training, I was taught a variety of abilities, including many of those listed above. During the course of our project's implementation, five key duties were defined.
    Launch. The scope of the project and its components are defined during the initial phase of the project. Moreover, if this phase is not completed entirely, the remainder of the project may be terminated, and if difficulties are not detected or avoided during this phase, this may result in insurmountable problems later on in the project's life cycle. During the project's initial phase, it is vital to have a complete understanding of the business environment and to put in place the necessary controls to keep the project on track. If there are any design defects in the project at this point, they should be identified and corrected right away. Because of this, we might claim that the foundation stage of the project is the most crucial stage of the project.     Planning. Following the completion of the start phase, the project is given a thorough design. In order to successfully manage risks and expenses during project implementation, the primary goal is to estimate the project's expenses and resources in advance of execution. During the planning stage as well as during the implementation stage, mistakes will be made that will have a substantial impact on the project's success. Although the fundamental stages of development have been completed, the project still requires extra fruitful labor.
    The following items have been completed in part, but they must be completed in their whole by the end of the preceding phases: Execution. Processes required to achieve the project requirements as defined in the project management plan are included in the Implementation phase of the project lifecycle. This stage comprises coordinating people and resources, as well as ensuring that their activities are consistent with the project management strategy, among other responsibilities. Products are created as a result of actions that are carried out in accordance with the project management strategic plan.
    Observation and control are required. It is the responsibility of the supervision and audit stage to detect and resolve issues that may occur over the course of the project's implementation, as well as to ensure that corrective actions and implementation controls are implemented as soon as possible once the issues are identified. The detection of deviations from the project management plan's implementation through monitoring and assessing project performance is a crucial benefit of this phase, and it should not be overlooked.
    Finishing. The term "closure" refers to the project's official completion.
